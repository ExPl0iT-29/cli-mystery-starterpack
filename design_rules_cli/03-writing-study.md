# Writing Study

## Writing Function In This Genre

From a writer’s point of view, the text in `clmystery` is doing support work, not carrying the whole experience.

Its job is to:

- establish premise quickly
- make the player care enough to investigate
- frame file systems as evidence systems
- keep hints functional
- stay out of the player’s way

This is disciplined writing.

## Tone Analysis

The repo uses a light pulp-detective tone:

- direct
- playful
- a little theatrical
- never overwritten

The phrase choices matter. The repo says things like "whodunit" and "gumshoe," which create flavor without requiring long prose scenes.

## Strength Of The Intro Writing

The introduction works because it does three things fast:

1. gives the player a role
2. gives them a mission
3. tells them exactly where to start

That is ideal for CLI games. Players need momentum more than lore.

## Strength Of The Hint Writing

The hints are operationally written.

They do not merely say:

- "try harder"
- "look near the witness"
- "think about the clue"

Instead they say:

- use `grep`
- inspect file structure with `head`
- extract a line with `head` then `tail`
- combine files and count matches

That is excellent instructional writing because it supports action.

## What The Repo Avoids Well

The writing avoids common failures:

- no giant lore dump before the first move
- no overexplained backstory
- no character monologues blocking gameplay
- no ambiguous instruction language

This restraint is a major reason the game survives repeated play as a teaching tool.

## How Character Writing Works Here

Most characters are not deeply dramatized. They are functional suspects, witnesses, or noise entries.

This is correct for the form.

In a CLI mystery, the writer should spend prose budget on:

- the incident
- a few witness voices
- a few memorable clue details

Not on giving 1,200 roster names personality.

## Reusable Writing Rules

- Introduce the premise in under a minute of reading.
- Write all operational instructions plainly.
- Use flavor words sparingly but consistently.
- Give important witness text distinct voice.
- Keep background records mostly utilitarian.
- Never let prose hide the interaction model.

## How To Write Better Successors

To improve on `clmystery` without breaking the form:

- make key witness voices more distinct
- give the incident file slightly stronger scene-setting
- keep hints crisp and command-oriented
- avoid turning the game into a novella

## Writing Takeaway

In this genre, the best writing is not maximal writing. It is precise, atmospheric, and operationally useful.
